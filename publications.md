<div class="publication" data-type="conference" data-year="2024">
  <h3>Cosine-Gated LSTM for Sequential Learning</h3>
  <div class="publication-meta">
    <strong>Goodluck Oguzie</strong><br>
    <em>2024 IEEE 5th International Conference on Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning (PRML)</em><br>
    2024, pp. 8-15
  </div>
  <p><strong>Abstract:</strong> We introduce a novel Cosine Gated LSTM (CGLSTM) architecture that uses cosine gating mechanisms for improved sequential learning. The architecture dynamically weighs new inputs versus hidden states based on similarities, enabling better handling of long-term dependencies. CGLSTM demonstrates superior performance in complex sequential data processing tasks, outperforming LSTM, GRU, and Transformer models in benchmark tasks including FallingBallEnv and SocNavGym.</p>

## PhD Thesis

<div class="publication" data-type="thesis" data-year="2024">
  <h3>Enhancing Robot Social Navigation with Reinforcement Learning and Advanced Predictive Models</h3>
  <div class="publication-meta">
    <strong>Goodluck Oguzie</strong><br>
    <em>PhD Thesis</em><br>
    Aston University, United Kingdom<br>
    <strong>Supervisor:</strong> Dr. Luis J. Manso<br>
    <strong>Associate Supervisor:</strong> Prof. Dr. Aniko Ekart
  </div>
  <p><strong>Abstract:</strong> This thesis presents novel approaches to social robot navigation by integrating reinforcement learning with advanced predictive models. The work introduces three key contributions: (1) Predictive World Models that enable multi-step ahead predictions, (2) Cosine-Gated LSTM (CGLSTM) architecture for improved sequential learning, and (3) Adaptive Prediction Horizons that dynamically adjust based on environmental complexity. The research demonstrates superior performance in simulation environments including LunarLander-v2 and SocNavGym, achieving higher success rates and more efficient navigation compared to baseline methods.</p>
